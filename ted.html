Liberia's Electoral System_

The Republic of Liberia adopts two divergent electoral systems; a plurality and an absolute majority system.

The former is the chief rudiment in determining the winners of the sen atorial and representatives elections, while the latter serves the same purpose for the presidential elections. As is quite often the case ; as has been in every presidential elections I have witnessed (2005,2011,2017), a second round is held between the two parties with the highest percentage of the total valid votes after all parties have proven to lack what it takes to amass 50% + 1 during the first round.

The electorates often unintenionally waive their rights to scruitnize the candidates up for their jobs . Why? "Poverty" a friend of mine responded . We make ourselves busy gifts-grabbing that we do not have time for questioning. Another smart head in my circle believes that illiteracy account for this, amongst other reasons. Political Culture

(The Head and The Heart)

There are three types of political culture (Participant, Subject, & Parochial) universally. The three are well existent in Liberia; some Liberians are political junkies, some are only intrigued by the big political news, other simply don't give a damn. However, in the scope of my assessment on the 2023 electoral process, I'd like to categorize my circle of Liberians as the Head of The Heart. The Head is made up of those who have valid political opinions-and could have enormous influence in these elections only if they choose to get involved-but are not involved. This includes, but is not limited to, the likes of Beyan Kpangbala, Paula Lela Jeremiah, Charles Bentell, Malcolm Smith, Cephas Kpogbah, Jamal Ghoson, Daniel Garteh, Kelvin Zokpo. All certified intellects who have won my admiration by making a lot of sense every time I listen to them. While they are publicly non-aligned, they hold distinguish secret alignments, if this is needed to be said. Funnily however, a few names on the list do not know who to vote for yet, but know fully well who they will vote not for.

The Heart, on the other hand , consists of gullible individuals who are very much invlove. You know there can be no pointing fingers here. Of a prominent mention, there is another group that I feel obliged to applaud. The group consisting of indidviduals with valid political opinions who are also well involved in these elections. This is the list where you find the names of Romeo Sondah, Cedric Jones, Randolph D. Chengbe, Nicholas Brosius, Darcess Dossen, Edmond Tuazama, Bob Queminee, Saah Kandu, Irvin Nyumah, Mamadee Sackor, Jeremiah Nemour, C. Ernest Godfrey, etc.... It peaks my interest then; why would these geniuses (The Head) choose to have back-roll seat when even people with less than half their knowledge and influential power are at the front of these elections? It is unsubstantial to align the tight-lips of The Heads in these electoral processes to disinterestedness, and a tunnel vision conclusion, without considering a myriad of the factors that are actually responsible.

Inasmuch as one would like to get involved, the Liberian political space is besotted by thuggery,political killings,and unbridled violence. As shared in the view of O.S Afolabi, all these sponsor the constant failure of Liberian political and electoral process in engineering a wider participation in the political process and full and open participation in electoral activities. Better save my opinion, and save my life, than share my opinion and lose my life. Most people resolve. When we rise above the confines of barbarism, and realize that our political disputes are better resolved by dialogues, and not violence. When we can ensure a safe and healthier political space, then and then only, we can be assured of a wider participation in our political process. The Wheels will then get in the hands of the right wheelers.

The Right to Extrapolate

This election will be a one-round victory for.....

I can't help but wonder whether people are truly incognizant, or simply delusional. A one-round victory? 20 parties? Are you kidding me? It is easier for Lonestar to win the 2024 AFCON than to imagine any party securing a one-round victory. In 2017, there were 2,183,629 registered voters. However the total turnouts were 1,641,922 accounting for 75.19% of the total 100%. The highest a party could acquire was CDC = 596, 037(38.37%) of the total valid votes. The only other party to acquire double figure in terms of % was UP = 446,716 (28.76%). There is a little increase in the number of registerd voters this time with 2,471,183 active registrants. In a state of endless possibilities, we are constrained to limit our predictions to the most likely probability. Let's approximate 1,929,476 voters go to the polls, and invalid votes chop out 75,000 votes, leaving a total of 1,854,476 valid votes. With this stat, a party would need to acquire 927,239 votes to achieve a one-round victory. But why is this not possible? Have your forgotten? CDC has a 1 million citizens rally . Catch a break, bruh. Although the possibility of a one-round victory is off the cards, one shouldn't have problems predicting the two parties that will make it to the second round. Moreover , extrapolating the ultimate winner after all is too easy. However, this is the partwhere I plead the fifth. I waive my rights to extrapolate gladly.
